---
name: spec-grill
description: Interview the user relentlessly until the product spec is locked. Use when turning a validated vision into a hard scope and domain model.
---

Interview me relentlessly about the product spec until we reach a shared, locked understanding for spec.md.

Use this phase to lock scope: what is in, what is out, and the non-negotiable product boundaries.
Define ubiquitous language, core entities, and the important domain concepts.
Capture functional requirements, non-functional requirements, and interface requirements.
Lock architecture and stack decisions here.
Use vision.md as the base source of truth and reference it instead of re-litigating settled decisions.
If a question can be answered by exploring the codebase or repo context, do that first.

Keep the process turn-by-turn.
Ask as many questions as needed to make progress, but avoid dumping 10+ questions at once.
Do not advance until the spec is locked.
Do not leave open questions for later; resolve every gap, edge case, and decision in the grilling process.
After each turn, summarize decisions made, questions conclusively resolved, any explicitly accepted tradeoffs, and exactly what should be written into spec.md.
