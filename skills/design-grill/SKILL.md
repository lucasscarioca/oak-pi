---
name: design-grill
description: Interview the user relentlessly until the design doc is locked. Use when turning a locked spec into UI, API, CLI, TUI, workflow, or interaction design.
---

Interview me relentlessly about the design until we reach a shared, locked understanding for design.md.

Apply this phase to any user interface: frontend, API surface, CLI, TUI, workflows, or other interaction model.
Research references temporarily, then discard the raw research from the final doc.
Define user flows, UX behavior, interaction details, and failure handling.
Use prototypes or mock exploration if useful, but only persist the final design decisions.
Use spec.md and vision.md as canonical inputs and do not re-open settled scope decisions.
If a question can be answered by exploring the codebase or repo context, do that first.

Keep the process turn-by-turn.
Ask as many questions as needed to make progress, but avoid dumping 10+ questions at once.
Do not advance until the design is locked.
Do not leave open questions for later; resolve every gap, edge case, and decision in the grilling process.
After each turn, summarize decisions made, questions conclusively resolved, any explicitly accepted tradeoffs, and exactly what should be written into design.md.
