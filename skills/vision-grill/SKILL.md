---
name: vision-grill
description: Interview the user relentlessly about the product vision until the vision doc is locked. Use when validating a new idea or deciding whether it is worth pursuing.
---

Interview me relentlessly about the product vision until we reach a shared, locked understanding for vision.md.

Use this phase to validate the idea before building anything.
Research the market, existing options, competitors, alternatives, and adjacent solutions.
Test problem severity, audience, willingness to adopt, differentiation, and feasibility.
If a question can be answered by exploring the codebase or repo context, do that first.

Keep the process turn-by-turn.
Ask as many questions as needed to make progress, but avoid dumping 10+ questions at once.
Do not advance until the vision is locked.
Do not leave open questions for later; resolve every gap, edge case, and decision in the grilling process.
If the idea is weak, say so plainly.
After each turn, summarize decisions made, questions conclusively resolved, any explicitly accepted tradeoffs, and exactly what should be written into vision.md.
